About Us

Youth for Environment And Social Transformation (YEAST) is a non-profit, non-governmental organization established to empower youth in driving environmental and social change. The organization focuses on bridging the gap between rural, environmental, and social education, aiming to equip students and local communities with the skills and knowledge necessary for sustainable development. YEAST operates in various sectors, including environmental conservation, social issues, education, and agriculture, by offering training programs, discussions, and awareness campaigns. Through its initiatives, YEAST promotes leadership development, sustainability, and social justice, particularly targeting youth to inspire them to become proactive change-makers. The organization also supports local farmers by introducing sustainable agricultural practices and enhancing their knowledge in modern farming techniques. Since its inception, YEAST has successfully conducted campaigns, implemented sustainable farming practices, and fostered community engagement, with a vision of creating a sustainable and equitable society where youth play a pivotal role in shaping the future.

Animal Health Camp

YEAST (Youth for Environment And Social Transformation) has been actively implementing Animal Birth Control (ABC) initiatives to promote animal welfare, curb the stray dog population, and prevent zoonotic diseases such as rabies. The ABC program involves the humane sterilization of stray dogs, followed by vaccination and release back to their original habitats. This scientifically proven method reduces overpopulation, minimizes human-animal conflicts, and enhances public health. YEAST collaborates with local municipalities, farmers, and volunteers to identify stray dog populations, organize ABC camps, and raise awareness about responsible pet ownership. Over the past years, YEAST has successfully sterilized and vaccinated over 1,800 dogs across various districts, including Arghakhanchi, Butwal, Palpa, Nawalpur, and Rupandehi. The program operates in seasonal phases, such as the Animal Birth Control & Anti-Rabies Vaccination Program in Butwal, where 186 dogs were sterilized in just three days. Despite challenges like logistical issues in rural areas and community misconceptions about sterilization, YEAST has worked with local governments to overcome these obstacles. The impact of the program includes reduced stray dog populations, lower rabies risks, and improved human-animal coexistence.
